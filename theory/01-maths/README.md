- Algebra

## **1. Number Theory**
- **Divisibility & GCD/LCM**: 
    Euclidean algorithm 
    Bézout’s identity 
    properties of gcd/lcm
- **Prime numbers**: 
    Infinitude of primes 
    fundamental theorem of arithmetic
- **Congruences**: 
    Modular arithmetic
    Fermat’s little theorem
    Euler’s theorem, Wilson’s theorem
- **Diophantine equations**:  
  - Linear: \( ax + by = c \)  
  - Pythagorean triples  
  - Pell’s equation \( x^2 - dy^2 = 1 \)  
  - Mordell equations (cubic, like \( y^2 = x^3 + k \)) in simple cases
- **Order modulo \( n \)**: 
    Primitive roots,
    exponent lifting lemma
- **Quadratic residues**: 
    Legendre symbol,
    Gauss’s lemma,
    quadratic reciprocity
- **p-adic valuations** 
    (Lifting the Exponent Lemma — LTE)
- **Chinese remainder theorem**
- **Functions on integers**: 
    floor function, 
    multiplicative functions, 
    Möbius inversion (basic use)
- **Techniques**: 
    Infinite descent,
    modular arithmetic constraints,
    bounding and case analysis

## **2. Combinatorics**
- **Counting principles**: 
    Bijection, 
    double counting, 
    pigeonhole principle, 
    inclusion–exclusion
- **Graph theory**: 
    Basics of trees,
    bipartite graphs, 
    matchings (Hall’s marriage theorem),
    Eulerian/Hamiltonian paths,
    planarity (Euler’s formula),
    coloring (vertex/edge),
    extremal graph theory (Turán type)
- **Recurrence relations & generating functions**: 
    Ordinary generating functions,
    Fibonacci/Catalan numbers,
    linear recurrences
- **Combinatorial designs & systems**: 
    Block designs,
    Ramsey theory (finite,
    basic: \( R(3,3)=6 \) etc.)
- **Extremal combinatorics**:  
  - Erdős–Ko–Rado type problems  
  - Sperner’s lemma  
  - Basic inequalities: AM-GM, Cauchy–Schwarz in combinatorics
- **Probabilistic method**: 
    Expectation, 
    Lovász local lemma (concept)
- **Invariants, monovariants, greedy algorithms, parity, invariants for games**
- **Plane/geometry combinatorics**: 
    Convex polygons,
    points/lines arrangements,
    incidence results

## **3. Geometry**
- **Triangle geometry**:  
  - Centers: centroid, orthocenter, circumcenter, incenter, excenters  
  - Ceva, Menelaus, Stewart’s theorem, mass points
- **Circle geometry**:  
  - Power of a point, radical axis, coaxial circles  
  - Cyclic quadrilaterals, Ptolemy’s theorem, inscribed angles
- **Similarity & homothety**: Spiral similarity, dilation
- **Trigonometry**: Law of sines/cosines, trigonometric identities, trigonometric Ceva
- **Coordinate geometry**: Analytic methods, barycentric coordinates, complex numbers (on plane)
- **Inversion** (geometric transformation)
- **Projective geometry basics**: Cross-ratio, harmonic bundles, perspectivity, Pascal/Brianchon theorems
- **Locus problems**
- **Collinearity & concurrency**: Theorems of Desargues, Pappus, radical center
- **Geometric inequalities**: Triangle inequality, Erdős–Mordell, isoperimetric ideas
- **Area method** (barycentric/areal coordinates)
- **Complete quadrilaterals**, symmedians, Apollonian circles, etc.

---

## **4. Algebra**
- **Polynomials**:  
  - Roots, Vieta’s formulas, symmetric polynomials  
  - Fundamental theorem of algebra (concept)  
  - Polynomial interpolation, Lagrange, remainder theorem  
  - Gauss’s lemma, Eisenstein’s criterion (for integers)
- **Functional equations**: Cauchy’s equations, injectivity/surjectivity methods, substitutions, using inequalities to bound
- **Inequalities**:  
  - AM-GM, Cauchy–Schwarz, Hölder, Chebyshev, Jensen’s inequality  
  - Rearrangement inequality, Muirhead (for symmetric sums)  
  - Schur’s inequality  
  - Tangent line method (Karamata, majorization concept)
- **Sequences & series**: Recursive sequences, convergence ideas (for bounded monotonic in olympiad context)
- **Real analysis basics**: Continuity, intermediate value property, monotonic functions (for functional equations)
- **Algebraic manipulations**: Summation, products, telescoping, factorization tricks

## **Additional Skills** (cross-cutting)
- **Proof techniques**: Induction (simple, strong, double), contradiction, construction, extremal principle
- **Algorithmic thinking**: Invariants, monovariants, greedy algorithms
- **Problem-solving strategies**: Symmetry, pattern finding, reduction to special cases, generating examples/counterexamples
